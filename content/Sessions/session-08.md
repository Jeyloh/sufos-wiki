## Summary

I drew from the Deck of Many Things.

I am writing that sentence first because if I do not write it first I will spend three pages avoiding it.

I drew two cards. The first removed two points from every ability I possess — strength, constitution, wisdom, everything, all of it, a across-the-board dimming of the person I am. The second showed a horned head, meaning unknown. And somehow the worst part of the first card is not the mechanical consequence. It is that it damaged my relationship with Moradin. I could feel it, the way you feel a crack forming in a load-bearing beam — not immediately dangerous, not catastrophic, but present, and wrong. I did this. I chose to draw.

The day had many other things in it. Important things. But they will all wait their turn. The Deck was the crack in the day's foundation.

## Key Events

[[emberstone|Emberstone]] inside the walls is, if anything, more impressive than outside. The carved stone passages between the cliff-buildings have been polished by centuries of foot traffic into a smooth, almost luminescent surface. I was running my hands along the walls as we walked, which the party has learned to ignore.

At the Temple of Joint Worship — a sensible concept, one building for the six major gods, reducing theological real estate competition — we met [[cheereen|Cheereen]], a paladin of Bahamut serving as city guard. She is investigating damage to two of the god statues outside: [[falang|Falang]] (god of travel) and [[paylore|Paylore]] (god of the sun). Vandalism, she said. Dragon Cult, she suspected. She mentioned a red dragonborn priest of Bahamut called [[ethergard|Ethergard]] who needed to be located. The city's military uses Bronze and Brass dragons — this is a significant defensive asset and also means that any red dragon presence is an immediate threat indicator.

[[zeffel|Zeffel]]'s establishment is through a covered market passage and into a compound that manages to be simultaneously modest and very clearly expensive. We passed the [[titanfall-arena|Titanfall Arena]] on the way — enormous beyond the city's apparent capacity to fill it, which means it was built for something else or for a time when this city was far larger. The scale of it is geological in its excess.

At [[zeffel|Zeffel]]'s compound we met [[helmer|Helmer]] (an archaeologist funded by [[estian|Estian]], which is an interesting arrangement), [[estian|Estian]] himself (older, well-connected, the kind of quiet power that surface merchant cities accumulate in their founding families), and [[kynos-gruun|Kynos Gruun]] (Red Cape leader, cold, arrogant, the particular kind of arrogant that tells you a person has been allowed to be arrogant for too long without consequence). There is apparently a structure of four merchants called Copperlords who run much of the city's actual governance: [[zeffel|Zeffel]], [[kynos-gruun|Kynos Gruun]], [[estian|Estian]], and an art dealer named [[onio|Onio]].

[[zeffel|Zeffel]] himself is a tiefling. Good one. I say "good one" not as a judgment on his species but as an assessment of his character — he is straightforward, generous by surface standards, and treated us as equals immediately, which I respect. He was in the middle of this assessment when four [[githyanki|Githyanki]] warriors came through the windows.

Then a young red dragon came through the wall.

His name was [[zephyrion|Zephyrion]]. He was young, angry, and clearly not acting freely — there was something controlling him, or had been, and whatever it was had been removed recently or partially, leaving him in a state of furious disorientation. We fought the Gith warriors, which was the more pressing immediate problem. The dragon was causing structural damage to what I had, moments before, been admiring as fine stonework. I tried not to take this personally.

When the Gith were dead, we had a dragon problem. The city's Brass dragon forces would be coming. A young red dragon in the city center with civilian casualties would not end well for [[zephyrion|Zephyrion]].

[[leo|Leo]] negotiated. I backed him up with presence — a Dwarven cleric of Moradin holding a fire-infused warhammer is not the worst thing to have visible when you are negotiating with a fire dragon. We offered [[zephyrion|Zephyrion]] a choice: accept freedom and owe us a single favor, or face the city's dragon defenders. He chose the favor. He left. Ungraciously, but he left.

This is, by the way, the correct outcome morally: a young creature who was being controlled made a bad decision under that control, and we gave him a path forward that did not require his death. Moradin does not ask me to kill everything that can be redirected. I was not certain the rest of the party understood that was what we were doing, but the result was the same.

We leveled up. Moradin acknowledged it. I felt it in my hands — the particular kind of power that deepens rather than expands, the way a vein of ore thickens as it descends rather than spreading shallower. ([[level-9|Level 9]], for the record.)

[[zeffel|Zeffel]] rewarded us five thousand gold and offered twenty thousand more contingent on two objectives: kill the [[mind-flayer|Mind Flayer]] hiding somewhere in the city, and negotiate with the [[githyanki|Githyanki]] in the [[underdark|Underdark]]. Both objectives happen to align with finding [[aradin|Aradin]] and uncovering the cult cell, so this is convergent rather than divergent.

[[egdalen-warendin|Egdalen Warendin]] is [[azar|Azar]]'s contact in the city — an information broker. We found him. We traded information for information. The most important piece: [[aradin|Aradin]] is in [[vultures-vault|Vulture's Vault]] prison, framed for theft at [[onio|Onio]]'s gallery.

And then someone produced the Deck of Many Things.

[[leo|Leo]] drew and gained power and a mask. [[murdoch|Murdoch]] drew and received the ability to change one fateful outcome. Then I was looking at the Deck.

I am a geologist. I understand that the conditions that created a formation are not the same as the formation itself — that what you see is always the result of forces you cannot directly observe. The Deck, I reasoned, is a set of conditions. Two draws. I extended my hand.

First card: the hex. Two points removed from all statistics. The crack in my connection to Moradin that I described above. I sat with this for a moment. I am still sitting with it.

Second card: a horned head. No immediate visible effect. I do not know what it did. I know it did something.

We went to [[vultures-vault|Vulture's Vault]]. Got inside, found [[aradin|Aradin]]'s cell. [[leo|Leo]] spoke to him in Thieves' Cant while a guard stood present — I do not know Thieves' Cant and I dislike this kind of communication that deliberately excludes others, but this was not the moment. [[aradin|Aradin]] hinted that the cell beside his had collapsed — a cave-in from [[embermines|Embermines]] below [[red-rock|Red Rock]] had demolished it. He was pointing us somewhere.

We ran for the mine entrance.

[[leo|Leo]] killed the guard on the way. The guard had rung his bell first. There will be pursuit.

We are in the mines now. I am writing this by the light of [[moltres|Moltres]], which burns steady even when I am not.

## Discoveries

- [[aradin|Aradin]] is in [[vultures-vault|Vulture's Vault]], framed by someone — likely connected to [[onio|Onio]]'s gallery theft. He is pointing us toward [[embermines|Embermines]] beneath [[red-rock|Red Rock]] via a collapsed cell.
- [[kynos-gruun|Kynos Gruun]] makes me uneasy. The kind of unease that a structural survey gives when the visible stonework is too perfect and you cannot see the foundations.
- The [[deck-of-many-things|Deck of Many Things]] was real and active. My draw: -2 to all stats. Unknown second effect. Relationship with Moradin damaged.
- [[zephyrion|Zephyrion]] owes us a favor. A red dragon is a dangerous creditor but a creditor nonetheless.
- The [[mind-flayer|Mind Flayer]] somewhere in the city is linked to the [[githyanki|Githyanki]] situation. These are connected threads.
- [[onio|Onio]] is the fourth Copperlord. The gallery theft that [[aradin|Aradin]] was framed for connects to him.

Moradin, I drew from the Deck because I thought I understood what I was doing. I probably did not understand what I was doing. The stone does not punish arrogance maliciously — it simply does what it does regardless of what I assumed. I accept the consequences. I will earn back what I have damaged.

Help me be stronger at reduced capacity than I was at full capacity. That is what you do for iron, after all: you fold it and hammer it and fold it again, and what comes out is steel. I will take being reduced, Moradin, if the folding makes me something better.

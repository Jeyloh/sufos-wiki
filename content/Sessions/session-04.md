## Summary

I sold a suit of full plate armor for two hundred gold pieces today. I did not know until [[leo|Leo]] told me, with a look on his face like he was watching someone use a Dwarven-carved gem to prop open a door, that it was worth sixteen hundred.

I shrugged. I did not need to carry it anymore, and I needed the coin. The party seemed more upset about it than I was, which tells you something about the different ways people assign value to things. To me, a thing is worth what it can be traded for in the moment you need to trade it. The market set the price. I accepted the market price. The armor is not my lost homeland. It is not [[moltres|Moltres]] or [[molton|Molton]]. It is metal I found in a dead man's storeroom. Let someone else carry it.

The Thornewood situation turned out to be considerably more dangerous than [[gordon-merek|Gordon Merek]]'s letter implied.

## Key Events

[[gordon-merek|Gordon Merek]]'s briefing laid out the situation clearly: [[walter-thornewood|Walter Thornewood]] holds a portal key that can reach [[three-waters|Three Waters]] — where his son [[felix-thornewood|Felix Thornewood]] apparently leads the settlement. Another son, [[stanley-thornewood|Stanley]], is reportedly somewhere in the Emberstone region, among Drow. The villa that Walter calls home had been reported as plague-stricken, which is why no one had checked on him recently.

Plague is one of those rumors that keeps people away very effectively. It is also, it turns out, an excellent cover for a hostile takeover.

Before we left, I prayed. Divination — simple, specific questions about the villa and what we would find there. Moradin answered as he always does: not with comfortable news, but with accurate news. The villa was occupied. It was a trap. Go anyway, but go knowing.

We went knowing.

The gates were manned by half-orcs who did not have the bearing of house servants. The villa's ostensible leader was a mercenary lieutenant called [[boar|Boar]] — a half-orc with a flat face and a comfortable contempt for everyone smaller than him, which included most of the party but not, for example, me when I am wearing my armor and holding [[moltres|Moltres]] and have the particular expression I am told I get when I am angry. We did not negotiate. We fought through every guard in the villa.

The stonework of the villa is old; pre-human construction that has been renovated badly, which is a particular kind of disrespect. The original foundations are sound. The additions are not.

We found [[stanley-thornewood|Stanley Thornewood]] alive, which was the objective. He had his infant son with him. His wife was dead. I will not write much about that beyond the fact that I said the appropriate words from the Moradin funerary rites over her, because she deserved that much and no one else in the room knew them.

[[leo|Leo]] also found three captured Drow in the cellar and freed them. I did not object to this. Whatever Drow are doing as prisoners in a half-orc mercenary's seized villa, they are prisoners, and Moradin does not ask me to leave prisoners to die because of their race. One was killed in the chaos that followed. Two survived and joined us.

[[boar|Boar]]'s actual plan was, when fully assembled, impressive in its cynicism: use a Scroll of Domination on the Drow captives, dress them in human disguises, send them to assassinate the queen's son, frame the Drow people for it, and ignite a war between humans and Drow. He was a tool, clearly — someone with strategic resources does not hire a lieutenant like Boar for the philosophical sophistication. Someone hired him for a specific operational job.

I killed [[boar|Boar]] in the end. His wizard lieutenant [[hollis|Hollis]] as well, from whom I looted a few useful items because a dead man's tools serve the living. [[stanley-thornewood|Stanley]] does not know where his father [[walter-thornewood|Walter]] is. That thread remains dangling.

The portal key worked. We stepped through it and arrived in [[three-waters|Three Waters]].

## Discoveries

- [[boar|Boar]]'s assassination plot was sophisticated beyond his apparent capacity — he was working for someone. The Scroll of Domination is not a common item. Someone with resources commissioned this operation.
- [[felix-thornewood|Felix Thornewood]] leads [[three-waters|Three Waters]]. The portal key reaches him directly. This is significant strategic access.
- [[stanley-thornewood|Stanley Thornewood]] is now with us (or at least safe). His wife is dead. His infant son is alive. His father's whereabouts are unknown.
- The freed Drow captives — two surviving — are now traveling with the party. I am watching them carefully. Not with hostility. With the kind of careful observation a geologist gives to an unfamiliar rock formation: without assumptions, with attention.

## Open Threads

- Who commissioned [[boar|Boar]]? The scale of resources involved (Scroll of Domination, hired mercenary force, villa takeover) suggests a significant organizing power.
- [[felix-thornewood|Felix Thornewood]] and [[three-waters|Three Waters]] — we are heading there now. What is the state of the settlement?
- The Earth Orb locations from my Divination remain the priority. We are moving toward [[three-waters|Three Waters]], but every day the orb locations could be shifting.

I find myself thinking more often about what comes after this. About what we are building toward. We are accumulating allies, intelligence, and complications in roughly equal measure, and the pile is getting tall.

[[moltres|Moltres]] caught the late afternoon light through the portal and glowed the way the warhammer does when it knows a fight is coming. I am not certain hammers know things. I am also not certain they do not. Moradin works in stone and metal and fire, and he has always found ways to speak through all of them.

[[three-waters|Three Waters]] awaits.

## Summary

I had what is apparently being called my "stag do" at some point around here.

I want to be clear that this is not how a Dwarven betrothal ceremony works. In the traditions of my people, the period before a wedding involves deep prayer, a ritual fast, the presentation of a geological survey of land the couple intends to build on, and the blessing of an elder cleric of Moradin. It does not involve whatever happened in [[emberstone|Emberstone]] with this party. I am choosing not to record the specifics. Edna will never read this, but still.

I love her. I miss her. I am trying to be worthy of coming home to her.

The day was otherwise a complicated accumulation of near-misses, moral compromises, and one genuinely good deed buried among several questionable ones. We are tracking the [[mind-flayer|Mind Flayer]]. We found its trail but not its body.

## Key Events

Emerging from [[embermines|Embermines]] into the [[emberstone|Emberstone]] daylight felt like being quarried — the sun here is more aggressive than I am accustomed to, particularly after time underground. The red stone of the formations threw it back from every direction.

[[ransell-bondiak|Ransell Bondiak]] was waiting. He is a halfling inspector attached to the [[red-capes|Red Capes]] — [[kynos-gruun|Kynos Gruun]]'s organization — and he had questions. Pointed ones. The kind of questions that a man asks when he already suspects the answers and is watching your face for confirmation. He wanted to know what we had been doing in the mines at the time of the alarm.

[[zeffel|Zeffel]]'s guard vouched for us, which tells me that [[zeffel|Zeffel]] had already spent money and political capital keeping us out of the [[red-capes|Red Capes]]' hands before [[bondiak|Bondiak]] arrived. [[zeffel|Zeffel]] is covering for us at significant cost to himself. When we met with him afterward, he was furious — not the hot fury of a man who has been betrayed, but the cold fury of a man who has made a business decision and is watching it become expensive. Guards had been killed in the mines. He had cleaned up the immediate consequences. He expected us to not create more.

I said nothing. I was thinking about [[leo|Leo]] and the guard at the mine entrance and the alarm bell and "do you always have to shoot first?" I am genuinely trying to understand whether this is a tactical disagreement, a moral one, or both.

The [[slave-market|slave market]] was next.

I will write this plainly: I do not endorse slavery. Moradin does not endorse slavery. The [[duergar|Duergar]] practice it and I have made my peace with the fact that not every Dwarven tradition is a good one. When the party identified three [[gnomes|gnomes]] at auction, I agreed to buy their freedom. This was the correct decision and I feel no ambiguity about it.

They turned out to work for [[mungo-murdoch|Mungo Murdoch]] — [[murdoch|Murdoch]]'s father, apparently. This was not information any of us had, and [[murdoch|Murdoch]]'s reaction to it was complicated in ways I did not try to analyze. I noted it and moved on.

[[leo|Leo]] stole something from one of the slave traders immediately afterward. I did not see it happen but I heard about it afterward from [[kael|Kael]]. I have added this to the mental ledger. I am not going to address it directly today because today had enough direct addresses and I am tired.

The [[afterlife-casino|Afterlife Casino]] is a remarkable architectural achievement. Multiple levels, carved into a natural void in the cliff face, with the ceiling left partially open to create a skylight effect that the interior lamps then amplify. The stone is polished to a reflective quality. Whoever designed this understood both geology and light and how they interact. I spent a significant portion of the visit examining the ceiling and walls.

[[murdoch|Murdoch]] gambled. Won some money. I did not gamble. I am not opposed to gambling on principle — Moradin does not have a position on dice games — but I find it uninteresting. I do not enjoy systems where the outcome is random. I prefer systems where the outcome is determined by the quality of the work.

The [[mind-flayer|Mind Flayer]] investigation led us to a disturbing discovery. The [[iron-ring|Iron Ring]] — a mercenary squad in the city — had vanished entirely just before ambassadors from [[kayryn|Kayryn]] were scheduled to inspect them. An entire unit, gone. No witnesses, no struggle, no explanation.

When we found their barracks, we found the unit. Or what remained of it.

They were walking. Blank-faced, unresponsive except with violence when approached, moving through the barracks space in purposeless patterns. I have read about this — it is what a [[mind-flayer|Mind Flayer]] does when it feeds: it does not always kill cleanly. Sometimes it takes what it wants and leaves the body running. The men were alive the way a cave that has had its ore extracted is still technically a cave — the structure is there, but what made it valuable is gone.

The barracks was full of traps. Carefully placed, well-constructed, designed to kill anyone who entered without knowing the layout. Someone had planned this space to be lethal to investigation. I cleared two traps myself and watched [[leo|Leo]] nearly lose a hand to a third.

Then the [[mind-flayer|Mind Flayer]] was there — albino, ancient-looking, moving with the particular certainty of a creature that knows exactly how dangerous it is. It appraised us. Decided we were not worth its immediate attention or, more likely, not immediately killable enough to be convenient. And teleported away.

Gone. No destination, no trail. Just gone.

## Discoveries

- The [[iron-ring|Iron Ring]] has been brain-drained by the [[mind-flayer|Mind Flayer]]. The soldiers are alive but empty. This was done to prevent them from being available for the [[kayryn|Kayryn]] ambassador inspection — the timing is too precise to be coincidence.
- The [[mind-flayer|Mind Flayer]] is albino, which may indicate an old specimen or a colony variant. It teleports with precision and has been operating in [[emberstone|Emberstone]] for long enough to establish prepared positions.
- [[zeffel|Zeffel]] is spending significant resources to cover for us. This is a debt we need to eventually repay in kind.
- [[mungo-murdoch|Mungo Murdoch]] has gnome operatives in [[emberstone|Emberstone]]. This suggests he has some kind of network or operation here. What?
- [[kynos-gruun|Kynos Gruun]] and his [[red-capes|Red Capes]] are watching us through [[bondiak|Bondiak]]. We are not as invisible in this city as we might prefer.

- The slave trader [[leo|Leo]] stole from. I will file this and return to it.

I am thinking about Edna tonight. Not sentimentally — practically. She is a woman of considerable common sense, and she would look at the past several days and say something simple and true that I am currently unable to articulate because I am too close to it. She has a gift for that: reducing geological complexity to the essential load-bearing fact.

The essential load-bearing fact is: I need to find the [[mind-flayer|Mind Flayer]], find [[aradin|Aradin]], and stay alive long enough to pursue the Earth Orb.

Everything else is sediment. Important sediment, sometimes, but sediment.

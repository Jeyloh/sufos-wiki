## Summary

We killed the [[mind-flayer|Mind Flayer]].

Its name was [[svuss|Svuss]], as we discovered from its journal. An old creature with a long history of careful predation and even more careful record-keeping. The journal was written in [[deep-speech|Deep Speech]] and [[undercommon|Undercommon]], which [[leo|Leo]] translated, and which contained information that has significantly expanded the map of what we are dealing with.

We also ended up in the [[underdark|Underdark]] fighting [[githyanki|Githyanki]] soldiers, and the session ended with an adult red dragon and its rider emerging from the dark. I am writing this during a pause between "nearly dying" and "finding out what happens next," which is a position I have become unfortunately practiced in.

The twenty thousand gold from [[zeffel|Zeffel]] is apparently one step closer.

## Key Events

The barracks again. Same dead-eyed soldiers moving in their purposeless patterns. We cleared the remaining threats — and there were several, including things [[svuss|Svuss]] had left as sentinels. [[moltres|Moltres]] burns with particular intensity in close quarters, which is its best environment. The fire-infusion that [[moltar|Moltar]] and I worked into the head responds to my will rather than just physical contact, and in tight corridors fighting the kinds of constructs [[svuss|Svuss]] favored, the ability to push sacred fire is more valuable than raw striking force.

I went to the temple to pray after the barracks clearing. Just a brief visit to the [[temple-of-joint-worship|Temple of Joint Worship]] — I lit a candle at Moradin's section. No Divination, no Commune. Just present. Moradin seems to accept this too; not everything needs to be a request.

[[svuss|Svuss]]'s journal, when [[leo|Leo]] translated it, was a document of careful evil. Two distinct sections:

First: a ritual using an amulet to extend the life of a group of Illithids who had been exiled from their colony. They enslaved [[duergar|Duergar]] smiths — my people's kin, or something close enough to make me deeply angry — to forge the amulet to masterwork quality, then killed the smiths and bound their life-force to the metal. This is what the amulet in the barracks is: it is built on murdered [[duergar|Duergar]] souls. I would very much like to undo this but I do not yet know how.

Second: a successful theft of a [[githyanki|Githyanki]] dragon scepter from their outpost beneath [[emberstone|Emberstone]], called [[crech-mediin|Crech Mediin]]. This scepter has three properties: it can locate red dragons specifically; it can call nearby ones; and it can dominate them. This is why the [[githyanki|Githyanki]] lost control of whatever dragon they had been riding — the scepter was stolen. There are apparently three such scepters in existence, carved from Tiamat's wisdom teeth, part of an ancient pact that may relate to how the [[githyanki|Githyanki]] freed themselves from [[mind-flayer|Mind Flayer]] control in the first place. The circle here is unpleasantly closed: [[svuss|Svuss]] used a tool created from an act of Githyanki independence to take away the independence of a Githyanki dragon.

[[svuss|Svuss]] could not use the scepter himself. It was enchanted specifically for [[githyanki|Githyanki]].

Most importantly: [[kynos-gruun|Kynos Gruun]] is mentioned by name as someone conspiring with [[svuss|Svuss]]. There it is. Written down, in a dead creature's careful script. I told you the stonework was too perfect.

We also found: a magical dagger, coins, a spellbook, a scroll of Wither and Bloom, and Gloves of Missile Snaring. Practical acquisitions.

[[leo|Leo]] woke screaming in the night. [[svuss|Svuss]] was using some kind of psychic pressure to demand its journal back. This clarified that [[svuss|Svuss]] was still alive and still wanted things. We found it — or it found us — and the party took its head. I will not be sentimental about it. [[svuss|Svuss]] built its extension of life on murdered souls. There is a reckoning that comes for everything that takes without permission, whether it takes ore from the earth without ceremony or life from a person without consent.

The dragon scepter is now in our possession. The [[mind-flayer|Mind Flayer]] amulet too.

We went back to [[zeffel|Zeffel]] with [[svuss|Svuss]]'s head and had a larger strategic conversation. The decision reached: negotiate with the [[githyanki|Githyanki]] directly. The head of the creature that stole their scepter, and the scepter itself, should buy us a conversation. Another adventuring group called the [[seekers|Seekers]] will handle the [[underdark|Underdark]] side of the investigation that we bungled (the [[iron-ring|Iron Ring]] situation was apparently messier than we presented it). We also discussed [[kynos-gruun|Kynos Gruun]] — positioning someone better as Copperlord in his place. [[estian|Estian]] or another option.

We also spoke to [[helmer|Helmer]] and [[estian|Estian]] at some point in here, gathering information and building the web of who knows what in this city.

Then into the [[underdark|Underdark]].

The [[githyanki|Githyanki]] were hostile. This was expected. What was not expected was the specific chaotic quality of the fight — they have abilities that create cognitive disruption, disorientation, that made holding a battle line genuinely difficult. I nearly died. I am writing "nearly died" the way I have been writing it in these logs with increasing frequency and I am starting to wonder if I am becoming inured to something that should alarm me more. I was down. [[murdoch|Murdoch]]'s magic pulled me back.

Then [[murdoch|Murdoch]] threw two of them into the lava. The [[underdark|Underdark]] here runs close to active magma channels — the heat had been increasing for some time, and the glow from below is visible through cracks in the basalt floor. Two Githyanki soldiers went into that glow. They did not come back.

Then a shadow on the ceiling became a shape, and the shape became an adult red dragon, and on its back was a rider.

## Discoveries

- [[svuss|Svuss]] is dead. [[zeffel|Zeffel]]'s twenty thousand gold is partially earned.
- [[kynos-gruun|Kynos Gruun]] conspired with the [[mind-flayer|Mind Flayer]]. Written confirmation. He is the enemy within the Copperlord structure.
- The dragon scepter and its properties: locate, call, dominate red dragons. Carved from Tiamat's wisdom teeth. Part of an ancient Githyanki independence pact. Crucially important.
- Three such scepters exist. We have one.
- The [[mind-flayer|Mind Flayer]] amulet is built on murdered [[duergar|Duergar]] souls. This requires addressing somehow.
- The [[githyanki|Githyanki]] outpost under [[emberstone|Emberstone]] is [[crech-mediin|Crech Mediin]]. We are now apparently heading toward it.

I know they were skilled — the masterwork quality of the amulet proves that — and that they died for that skill because something decided their lives were worth less than the product of their hands.

Moradin, put their craftsmanship to use. Whatever is bound in that metal, let it serve the work they would have chosen, not the work that was forced on them.

## Summary

I have read accounts of underground structures that defy explanation — places where the earth holds things that should not exist, preserved by depth and pressure the way pressure preserves everything, eventually. [[drageslottet|Drageslottet]] is such a place. I have now been inside it. I wish I could say I understood it better for the experience.

What I understand is that the Dragon Cult is more organized and more dangerous than my Earth Ashari briefings suggested. What I understand is that we nearly died through a combination of recklessness — mine included, I will own that — and that Moradin apparently has work for me yet, because I am still breathing.

I also understand that something is coming. I saw it in a dream with such clarity that it might as well have been carved in granite.

## Key Events

We descended from the elevator into the heart of the castle. The architecture is unlike anything I have catalogued. The stone is not native to this region — it has been imported or transmuted, which is an extraordinary undertaking. Whoever built this place had power over stone that I respect even as I despise what they used it for.

We moved carefully through the upper galleries until we heard voices. Through a grate in the floor, we overheard a [[drow|Drow]] commander — [[zuthan|Zuthan]] — briefing his officers. He spoke of [[driders|Driders]], which are what Lolth makes of Drow warriors who fail her: spider below the waist, Drow above, punishment and weapon at once. He also spoke of a planned attack on [[three-waters|Three Waters]] using a flying ship.

Here is where the party made a decision I did not fully agree with but could not argue against: we chose not to sabotage the ship. If we had, we would have alerted every soldier in the castle to our presence. It was tactically sound. It felt morally insufficient. But I recorded my objection and said nothing more about it.

We then encountered a Kobold merchant in one of the lower passages — [[mglukk|M'Glukk]] — who had set up a small trade post in the chaos of the castle the way certain fungi establish themselves in cracks: efficiently, without being asked. He traded us information about the castle layout in exchange for coin and a few items from our packs. He also revealed a hidden stash of goods he'd accumulated — stolen or salvaged, hard to say. Useful either way.

We disguised ourselves as Dragon Cult members and moved through the halls. The guards were not perceptive. Low morale, low training — this is an army assembled by ideology rather than discipline, and it shows in how they stand, how they challenge, how they respond to a confident stride and a cult emblem.

We learned something critical: the cult possesses only the Earth Orb so far. The other three Elemental Orbs are still unaccounted for by them. We also learned — I am writing this down because I need to not forget it — that when they acquire a second orb, they converge on a place called [[blade-garden|Blade Garden]]. That is where we need to be when it happens.

Then we made our first catastrophic error.

In one of the deep halls, we encountered a woman imprisoned in a magical cage. She was clearly a devil — the energy around her was unmistakable, the kind of spiritual pressure that presses against a cleric's senses the way heat presses against skin near a forge. She offered us a trade: she would grant a Wish to whoever freed her, in exchange for the right to collect their souls upon death.

None of us accepted the trade. I am proud of that much.

But then [[leo|Leo]] freed her anyway.

She walked out of that cage and into the Great Hall and killed every guard in it. I watched from a distance and felt sick. Not because the guards did not perhaps deserve it — they were Dragon Cult, they had made their choices. But because we had just unleashed something evil to do our work for us, without a contract, without a leash, without any guarantee of what she would do next. That is not Moradin's way. That is the way of people who tell themselves the outcome matters more than the method.

I said my piece. No one much wanted to hear it.

[[leo|Leo]] then decided to sneak into the Great Hall after her. I — and I confess this freely — decided that if he was going in, I would go in with him and not sneak about it. I walked in. Upright. Warhammer visible.

This was deeply, profoundly stupid.

[[dolan-ward|Dolan Ward]] was there. I had heard the name spoken like a curse in the castle's lower levels. Now I understood why. He is not a powerful man in the way powerful men usually are — loud, armored, obvious. He is powerful the way certain geological formations are powerful: quietly, completely, in ways you only understand when you are already inside them. He nearly killed me. Leo and [[murdoch|Murdoch]] fled. I went down.

When I regained consciousness, we were in a cave in the walls of the castle — a natural void in the rock that I shaped into something defensible using the stone itself. My hands were shaking. Not from fear. From shame.

We took a long rest there.

## Discoveries

- The Dragon Cult holds only the Earth Orb. The other three orbs have not yet been seized. Time exists, but it is finite.
- When the cult acquires a second orb, they move to [[blade-garden|Blade Garden]]. This is their convergence point.
- [[dolan-ward|Dolan Ward]] (referred to also as "Grim" by some cult members) is extraordinarily dangerous. We are not ready to fight him directly.
- The [[drow|Drow]] under [[zuthan|Zuthan]] are planning to attack [[three-waters|Three Waters]] by flying ship. We did not stop the ship.
- A devil — species uncertain, imprisoned by Dolan Ward — was freed by our party. She killed a hall full of guards. We have no idea where she went.

**The Dream:**

I must record this carefully, because Moradin does not send idle visions.

During the long rest, I dreamed of four orbs arranged in a perfect square — the geometry of a load-bearing structure, the kind of symmetry that holds a vault up. In the center of the square was a hole. I looked down through it and saw two things simultaneously:

Tiamat, below. Five heads, each a different dragon color, moving like tectonic plates — slow and inevitable and crushing.

And Moradin, also below. Fighting her. Not winning, not losing. Fighting.

The four orbs are not just objects of power. They are structural. They are holding something back, or they are keys to something that has been locked. The dream was not subtle about this. Four points, a center, something below.

I need to find the Earth Orb. I need to find all of them.

## Open Threads

- [[dolan-ward|Dolan Ward]] is the most dangerous enemy we have encountered. We fled. That is the correct decision and I am still angry about it.
- The dream vision of Moradin fighting Tiamat. Four orbs. A hole. This needs to be understood.
- The devil woman is loose somewhere. We made this happen, however unintentionally. I am not at peace with it.
- The flying ship attack on [[three-waters|Three Waters]] is coming. We have not warned anyone.
- [[ozniak|Ozniak]] has still not been found.

I shaped stone tonight with trembling hands and prayed for steadiness. Moradin gave me the cave. He always gives me the cave — shelter in the rock, safety in the deep earth. I am grateful. I am also aware that I cannot hide in caves forever.

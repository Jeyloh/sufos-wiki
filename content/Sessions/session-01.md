## Summary

I was traveling alone, which is not unusual for a geologist on survey work. The road east of the river valley had exposed strata I had not seen before — a banded limestone with dark intrusions, possibly hydrothermal in origin. I had stopped to chip a sample when I heard the sound of combat ahead.

Three Kenku surrounded a group of armed humans. The humans were holding their own but losing ground. I do not leave good people to die in the road if I have fists and a warhammer to offer. I waded in.

The humans called themselves the [[murdoch-saints|Murdoch Saints]]. Motley bunch. A man with too much shadow about him. Another who moved like a blade looking for a sheath. We killed two of the Kenku. The third — their leader, a one-eyed bird calling himself [[roko|Roko]] — threw down his weapon and asked to speak.

What he showed me stopped my heart: a knife bearing the sigil of the Dragon Cult. The same cult that raided Dhildhor. The same cult that stole the Earth Orb from my people.

Moradin does not deal in coincidences. He deals in stone: slow, sure, and shaped by forces that have been building for ages. This meeting was a seam of ore running through otherwise ordinary rock. I knew I had to follow it.

## Key Events

Roko told us that his Kenku boss — [[ozniak|Ozniak]] — and the Dragon Cult's man [[oderman|Oderman]] were both guests of [[lord-therris|Lord Therris]] at his nearby villa. Roko wanted out. He hated the cult, hated Ozniak, and was willing to help us clear the villa in exchange for safe passage afterward. I am not a man who trusts easily, but his fear was not performance. I agreed.

We breached the villa. Roko was as good as his word — he pointed us to hidden rooms, identified guards, stayed back when the steel came out. We freed the human prisoners Oderman had penned in the upper rooms: common people, nothing special about them except that they were frightened and grateful. I sent a prayer for each of them as we moved them toward safety.

Oderman himself we found in the main hall. I will not detail the fight at length. He died. That is sufficient.

The cellar was worse. Lord Therris was dead down there, pinned to a ritual circle with his legs removed below the knee. A Dragon Cult working — I have read enough of their methods to recognize it. The stonework of the cellar was old and good, Dwarven-influenced by the look of the arches, and it felt obscene that such craft had been used to house that kind of evil.

[[ozniak|Ozniak]] had already fled. Someone tipped him off or he simply had better instincts than Oderman. Either way, he was gone, and the trail led to [[rumble-mines|Rumble Mines]].

We sent Lord Therris's daughter with the freed commoners to the Saints' tavern — someone had to do it, and she was barely twelve years old and white-faced with shock. Then we went to the mines.

The mines are deep and old. The stone here is sandstone over granite, which is unusual — the sandstone should not be here, geologically speaking. There are forces at work beneath this region that predate any human settlement.

That became clear when we descended far enough to see what was embedded in the earth.

A castle. Not a ruin, not a foundation — a complete castle, half-swallowed by the living rock, with its towers still rising upward and its depths plunging into genuine blackness. The locals apparently call it [[drageslottet|Drageslottet]]. The Dragon Castle. It has been there long enough that the stone has grown around it the way stone does: slowly, completely, without mercy.

There was an elevator. We took it down.

## Discoveries

- The Dragon Cult responsible for stealing the Earth Orb from Dhildhor is active in this region. This is the same organization, confirmed by Roko's identification of their sigil.
- [[ozniak|Ozniak]] (Kenku boss) has fled to or into [[rumble-mines|Rumble Mines]].
- [[drageslottet|Drageslottet]] exists — a Dragon Castle embedded in the earth beneath Rumble Mines, deep enough that it must predate any known civilization in this area.
- Lord Therris was killed in a Dragon Cult ritual. The nature of the working (removal of the legs) suggests something to do with binding or sacrifice. I need to research this.
- There is a letter somewhere among Oderman's effects referencing "the eighth month" and a war between good and evil. I did not get to read it in full. The party took it. I need to ask about it.

## Open Threads

- [[ozniak|Ozniak]] escaped. He knows we hit the villa. He will warn someone.
- The Dragon Cult is confirmed present and active in this region, which means their hunt for the four Elemental Orbs is underway. The Earth Orb is still missing. My mission remains unchanged.
- What is [[drageslottet|Drageslottet]] exactly? Who built it? It is not Dwarven work — the proportions are wrong, the stone-shaping method is wrong. But it is very old.

I find myself grateful for the Saints, strange as they are. I was traveling alone because the work of an Earth Ashari geologist is often solitary. I am beginning to think it should not be.

Moradin, give me patience with these surface-folk. And give me wisdom to know what I will find below.

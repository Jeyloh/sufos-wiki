## Summary

Two years.

I do not think the others fully understand what two years among the Fire Ashari means for a Hill Dwarf who spent his formative geological years studying cold stone in the deep dark. It means I understand heat differently now. It means I know how [[moltar|Moltar]] thinks, how [[azar|Azar]] commands, how a fire-pledge works and what it asks of you. It means I have [[moltres|Moltres]] and [[molton|Molton]] — not approximations of those weapons, not prototypes, but the finished things, fire-infused and properly made, consecrated in the Fire Ashari lodge with [[moltar|Moltar]]'s hands alongside mine and Moradin's blessing spoken over the cooling metal.

It means I am engaged to Edna.

Two years is enough time for a person to become someone slightly different. I am still devout. I am still a geologist. I am still proud of my people, and still in pain about [[prides-call|Pride's Call]], and still deeply uncomfortable with the cavalier way this party treats living beings who stand in its way. But I am also, I think, more patient than I was. Fire teaches patience the way stone teaches endurance: by punishing the lack of it immediately and without mercy.

The party is airborne now, sailing toward [[emberstone|Emberstone]] on a ship that I am not convinced should be airborne, commanded by a man I am not convinced should be commanding anything airborne.

## Key Events

The reunion at [[three-waters|Three Waters]] was brief by necessity. [[felix-thornewood|Felix Thornewood]] gave us use of an airship — a captured or acquired vessel, well-built but newer than I would like for extended travel. Air travel makes me uneasy in ways I have not fully philosophized yet. Stone does not move. I have spent my entire life in relationship with stone. Moving through the sky means moving through nothing, which is deeply architecturally unsound.

Our new captain: [[cedric-krast|Cedric Krast]]. Half-orc. Twirly mustache. The mustache is doing a great deal of work to establish personality. He is, I am told, an excellent sea captain — "excellent" meaning he has survived the sea for a substantial period, which is a limited form of credential when one is now navigating air. His own assessment is that an airship is "basically a ship, but up." I wrote this down so that when something goes wrong, I have documentation of how we got here.

New to the party: [[kael|Kael]], the ship's scout. I have not formed a full opinion of him yet. He moves quietly and makes useful observations. That is a beginning.

[[murdoch|Murdoch]] was discovered in the hold, sleeping off what appeared to be several days of determined drinking. [[leo|Leo]], in Murdoch's absence, had apparently read his private investigation notes. I expressed my disapproval of this and was ignored, which is becoming a habit.

[[elris|Elris]] received a letter that alarmed me more than I let on. Someone named [[castor|Castor]] has built a cult around him — around [[elris|Elris]], specifically — believing him to be a prophet of [[tharizdun|Tharizdun]], the Chained God. The Chained God is exactly as unpleasant as his title implies. A cult of his believers orienting around one of my traveling companions is the kind of development that keeps a cleric awake at night performing inventory checks on the status of his soul.

The party renamed the ship "Falsus' Vengeance." [[elris|Elris]] wrote it on the hull. He spelled "Vegans." This is the level of precision we are operating with.

We were attacked by a [[kayryn|Kayryn]] military airship. The patrol was professional, their ship was better armed than ours, and the fight was serious until [[kael|Kael]] proved his worth and [[elris|Elris]] solved the problem by throwing two officers over the side. I noted this for the ongoing mental ledger I keep of the party's conflict resolution methods. The surviving crew told us [[kayryn|Kayryn]] has four to six patrol ships in the region. We decided to push through anyway rather than retreat or detour.

A [[tharlia|Tharlia]] warship intercepted us shortly after. [[captain-anlow|Captain Anlow]] and his first mate [[lavant|Lavant]] boarded us. Professional. Courteous. They took the captured [[kayryn|Kayryn]] ship off our hands as a prize and handed us 1750 gold on the spot, with 7250 more redeemable at the [[emond|Emond]] court. I respect a man who pays cash immediately without negotiation. That is a man who understands the value of a motivated ally.

Then [[azar|Azar]]'s Sending reached me, which cut through the social and financial activity like a cold chisel through limestone: *Stay low. Aradin reported intel on Tiamat cult cell in Emberstone before he disappeared. His information is the priority.*

[[aradin|Aradin]]. An Earth Ashari agent I know by reputation. If he disappeared in [[emberstone|Emberstone]] while carrying intelligence about the cult, that intelligence died with him or is buried somewhere in that city. Either way: [[emberstone|Emberstone]] first, orbs second, everything else third.

## Discoveries

- [[azar|Azar]] has confirmed that [[aradin|Aradin]], an Earth Ashari agent, went missing in [[emberstone|Emberstone]] after gathering intelligence on a Dragon Cult cell there. Finding [[aradin|Aradin]] — or his information — is now a direct mission from Fire Ashari leadership.
- [[kayryn|Kayryn]] patrols this air region with four to six ships. We are operating in contested airspace.
- The 7250gp reward at [[emond|Emond]] court is pending and real. Worth remembering.
- [[elris|Elris]] has a cult building around him in [[thillion|Thillion]] centered on [[tharizdun|Tharizdun]] worship. This is either a coincidence or it is not a coincidence, and neither option is comfortable.

And I will find [[aradin|Aradin]].

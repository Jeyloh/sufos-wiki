## Summary

I have seen a great many impressive things in my life. The lode-veins of Dhildhor, running gold and silver and copper in layers that took eight hundred years to form. The forge-chambers beneath Fire Ashari's station, where the heat comes from something older than any intentional fire. [[drageslottet|Drageslottet]], embedded in the earth like a buried truth.

[[emberstone|Emberstone]] belongs on that list.

We have not yet entered its gates. We are camped at the edge of the approaches, the ship secured to three anchoring posts that [[cedric-krast|Cedric Krast]] drove into the sand with a look of immense confidence I have decided to find reassuring rather than alarming. Tomorrow we go in. Tonight I am writing.

## Key Events

The approach to [[emberstone|Emberstone]] took us over open desert — a landscape I have studied in surveys but never moved through for extended periods. Desert stone tells a different story than mountain stone. Everything here is about erosion: wind and heat and the occasional catastrophic flood that comes once in a generation and reshapes everything. The formations we passed over were remarkable — mesa structures with horizontal banding you could read like pages, each layer a different climate period, a different story of what the world was before humans arrived to name things.

I ate a small piece of gypsum I found on the deck from a previous cargo. It is tasteless and crumbles cleanly. A good mineral. I have been trying to limit the snacking when the party can see me, as it prompts questions I find tedious to answer.

The caravan appeared below us midday — six wagons spread across a trade road, with a mass of figures around them that resolved on closer inspection into ogres. Eleven of them, large and stupid in the characteristic ogre manner, tearing through the wagons with the specific enthusiasm of creatures who know they are too big to be effectively opposed.

One was not like the others. Light blue skin. Eyes that tracked us intelligently when we came in low. When it raised its hands, the air changed the way air changes before a lightning strike — that particular pressure that my mine-survey training associates with electrical hazards in enclosed spaces. An Ogre Mage. I called out a warning to the party.

The fight was not clean. Ogre Mages operate by creating confusion — illusions, paralysis, sleep effects — while the ordinary ogres press the physical attack. I held a position near the lead wagons and kept the ordinary ogres off the caravan guards while the rest of the party focused on the Mage. [[moltres|Moltres]] is not subtle but it is effective, and a Dwarven cleric who has planted his feet is harder to move than people expect. We brought the Mage down eventually.

The caravan leader was a human named [[arathan|Arathan]] — weathered, competent, with the specific kind of gratitude that a man who has watched his livelihood nearly destroyed feels when it is not quite destroyed. He offered us silk from the wagons, which I declined because I have nowhere to put silk and no use for it, but the others accepted pieces.

More importantly: he works for a tiefling merchant in [[emberstone|Emberstone]] named [[zeffel|Zeffel]]. He said he would tell [[zeffel|Zeffel]] that we saved his goods. In a city I know nothing about, with no local contacts and a missing Earth Ashari agent to find, having a merchant owe us a favor is worth more than silk.

Then we crested the last ridge before [[emberstone|Emberstone]] and I stopped writing for a while.

Three red rock formations — not mesas, not cliffs, something between: massive, rounded at the tops, showing iron oxide banding in deep reds and oranges that told me about ancient seabed, about oxidation events, about water that had not been here for a million years. The formations cradled the city the way a pair of cupped hands cradles water. Buildings in the rock faces, carved into the stone itself and painted in whites and terracottas. Buildings on top of the formations, connected by bridges. High walls all around the base, well-maintained, with patrol walks. Beyond the walls, the lowlands: tents, camels, trade goods spread across the sand in the organized chaos of a serious marketplace.

The stonework in the carved faces is extraordinary. These were not built — they were excavated. Someone looked at a red rock formation a thousand years ago and decided the building was already inside it, waiting to be released. That is the correct understanding of stone. I felt an immediate respect for whoever made those decisions.

The iron-rich sandstone would present challenges — it is softer than granite, more vulnerable to moisture damage, which explains some of the visible repairs in the lower walls. But the scale of the original vision is impeccable.

I am going to enjoy examining this city, when I am not hunting a missing Earth Ashari agent and trying to stop a dragon goddess from seizing control of the world's elemental foundations.

## Discoveries

- [[zeffel|Zeffel]] is a powerful merchant in [[emberstone|Emberstone]] and [[arathan|Arathan]]'s employer. We arrive with goodwill already established — a useful beginning.
- [[emberstone|Emberstone]] is built into and on top of three iron-rich sandstone formations. The architecture is predominantly excavated rather than built. This is a significant engineering tradition.
- Ogre Mage presence on the trade roads suggests that the roads to [[emberstone|Emberstone]] are not well-patrolled or that whoever patrols them has lost control of this stretch. Worth noting.
- The desert geology here is deep Cenozoic — I found an ammonite fragment in the camp soil tonight. This area was ocean floor. The iron content in the rock is a secondary oxidation product. I have taken notes.

I found a fragment of red jasper in the sand by the anchor posts tonight. I am keeping it. Jasper is a form of quartz — silicified mudstone, essentially — and the fact that it appears here in this color tells me there was once hydrothermal activity in this desert that has since cooled entirely. The activity is long over. The record of it is not.

Everything geological leaves a record, if you know how to read it.

I am going to find [[aradin|Aradin]]. And then I am going to find the Earth Orb.

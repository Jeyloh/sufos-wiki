# Session 15 — Tunnels Beneath the Trade

## Summary

Moradin gave me back what I had wagered, and so I began this day on my knees in gratitude — not on the back of any dragon, not in any hall of power. Just on my knees. By the end of the day I had stood in the chambers of cult leaders, walked under the streets of [[emberstone|Emberstone]] in tunnels I never knew existed, and ended a man's fevered double life with a spell. We thought we were chasing a Mind Flayer's ghost and a copper lord's greed. We were, in truth, walking into the room where a god is being undone and a demigod is being raised.

We are three of us now, with [[shereen|Shereen]] beside us. [[ryth|Ryth]] is not here. The deal we have come to stop is happening today. And as we left the temple of [[bahamut|Bahamut]], [[dolan-ward|Dolan]] was standing in the doorway with five more behind him, scepter raised at our paladin friend.

I am writing this knowing the next words I write may be from a less hopeful place.

## Key Events

### Morning — Murdoch's torment, the exorcism, the Wish

We woke to find that [[murdoch|Murdoch]] had a hard night. [[orgaz|Orgaz]] had been working through him in his sleep — I can see it in his hands when he wakes from those nights. [[leo|Leo]] had been out of the room: he had spent the dark hours scouting [[gruun-spire|Gruun's spire]] and had located the office where the trade meeting will likely be held. A useful and brave thing, but I file it away — the morning is for healing.

I sat with Murdoch and asked him plainly how he was. He answered honestly. I felt Orgaz himself reach toward me as I spoke — *touch* me, the way a cold thing presses through cloth. I made my saving throw against him with everything I had (a perfect roll plus my full Wisdom — Moradin's hand on the back of my neck) and threw myself at Murdoch with a Greater Restoration in my hands. I tried to drive Orgaz out.

I did not succeed. Orgaz remains.

But the spell did *something*. It cleared Murdoch's exhaustion. And it returned to him every memory he has lost while Orgaz wore his skin. He remembered, all at once, every person he had killed — in many cases the very people he had been hunting as a detective, the unsolved cases that had haunted his career. Murdoch *was* the Poetic Murderer. He had been chasing himself for years.

He stood up shaking. He told me, with great fear, that he is afraid Orgaz is the source of his magic — that to give him up is to give up his power. I did not have an answer for him. I told him that Moradin's path is built one stone at a time and that he did not have to decide today.

### Breakfast with Zeffel

[[leo|Leo]] and I went to breakfast with [[zeffel|Zeffel]]. He laid down a hard rule before we ate anything: under no circumstance can it ever come out that he is conspiring against [[kynos-gruun|Gruun]]. We agreed.

We told him about [[dolan-ward|Dolan Ward]]. We raised the theory that Dolan may be the central figure who has set the [[kayryn|Kayrun]] deal in motion. Zeffel listened.

He gave us in return:
- [[onio|Onio]] has a woman at his gallery — a curator named [[kiara|Kiara]] — who has frequent private contact with Gruun. She is our way in.
- [[gruun|Gruun]] is on full guard during the day, with [[the-seekers|The Seekers]] at his shoulder, and his nerves are tight as a bowstring until the trade closes.
- His read on [[eshian|Eshian]]: neutral, easy to manipulate, but smart. *Easy to manipulate but smart* — these are not contradictory in the way it might first sound. It is exactly the warning I would give about a wizard who has lived a long time in a city of liars.
- He warned that we cannot approach Onio openly about Kiara — Onio might connect the dots back to a move on Gruun and tip his hand.

He also raised a new factor: [[shereen|Shereen]]. The woman who can take dragon form and protects Emberstone. She does not interfere in politics — but she will move heaven and earth for the city itself. And she does not like red dragons. *Important if we ever bring [[zephyrion|Zephyrion]] / [[garax|Garax]] into the city skies.*

### Murdoch's Wish

We came back to Murdoch's room. He was standing, swaying, *very* drunk. He told us he was going to fix the problems. He asked if we wanted to see a magic trick — *it's gonna fix everything*, he said. He pulled a card from his pocket — from the [[deck-of-many-things|Deck of Many Things]], I am almost certain — and he cast Wish.

> *"I wish that Orgaz never possessed me."*

The world bent. I felt it the way I feel Moradin's name spoken aloud in the deep places.

The Poetic Murderer no longer exists. The cases Murdoch had built his identity around — they are gone from the world. The victims he killed in his sleep — they walk somewhere out there alive. Murdoch remembers fewer murders by the man he was hunting because that man, in the most literal sense, did fewer murders. Murdoch is no longer an alcoholic — that hunger of his, too, was downstream of the demon.

And Orgaz himself? I cannot answer that yet. Wish is a strange power and the wording was specific. The demon was not driven out — but his foothold in Murdoch's life has been retroactively pulled out from under him. We will see what crawls back.

### Plan: hit Onio's gallery

We agreed: Murdoch and Leo would disguise themselves with magic, walk into Onio's gallery, and approach Kiara as wealthy vineyard owners visiting town who needed an audience with Gruun. [[ryth|Ryth]] and I would stand outside as overwatch.

### A note from a dead man

Before we could move, we were intercepted. A slave boy — he gave his name as **[[jorek|Jorek]]** — pressed a folded note into my hand. He said it was from a half-elf who had been killed in the [[titanfall-arena|arena]] some days ago. The half-elf had no name attached.

I opened the note in private. It read:

> *"Asken glemmes ei."*
> Signed: **CCC**, beside a **red five-pointed star**.

*The ash is not forgotten.* It is an Ashari phrase — *asken*, ash; *Ashari*, the people of ash. The choice of words felt like authentication: a fellow Ashari reaching out. I sent immediately to [[azar|Azar]].

His answer came back short and devastating:

> *"Sufos — endelig resultater. Ukjent ledetråd. Synd med Aradins død. Dere kan komme tilbake dersom dere ikke har funnet mer om denne kulten — hører fra deg."*

Two things. First, [[aradin|Aradin]] is dead. Confirmed. The Fire Ashari agent we have been chasing the trail of — gone. Second, Azar does not recognize CCC or the red star either. Which means the letter signed CCC did not come from any Fire Ashari that Azar knows. Someone else is using the Ashari authentication phrase to reach me.

A name surfaced from the day's earlier conversations: **Carl's Caravan Company** — *CCC*. A location on the city map. Suddenly the geography of the puzzle shifted under our feet. The signature was not just a code. It was an address.

### Into Carl's Caravan Company

We changed plans on the spot. Onio's gallery would wait. We went to the CCC area of Emberstone and found a warehouse there that wore its innocence too tightly. We got inside.

A guard inside the warehouse stopped us. He asked, with the air of a man running a passphrase: *do you know number two?* We answered yes.

We did not, at the time, know who *number two* was. The bluff worked.

A thin man with a goatee began following us through the corridors — not openly, just present. We moved deeper. Found a chest marked with the same red five-pointed star from the letter. Beside it: a ladder going down. We took the ladder.

Below the city, in tunnels none of us knew existed, [[leo|Leo]] put two cultists in the dirt with sneak attacks before they could shout. We listened at a door.

### Helmer

Inside the room, [[helmer|Helmer]] was speaking. Our archaeologist. The man we escorted back from the [[crech-mediin|Crech Mediin]] mission. The man who studies under [[eshian|Eshian]] at the Academy.

He was saying that two orbs had been delivered. He was saying that this cell was responsible for the last orb — the **Fire Orb**.

I did not need to understand any more than that. We broke in. Sufos's battle cry: *take this fireball*. Murdoch took the cry literally and led us in casting Fireball.

The fight was brutal. Helmer had a half-dragon at his side and three cultists. We spent everything — spell slots, stamina, [[murdoch|Murdoch]]'s reserves — and we came out the other end with three of our enemies dead and Helmer alive on the floor in front of us. We did not have the resources for any more enemies. We had been very lucky.

We looted the room: a +1 Greatsword, studded leather, spell scrolls, spell ingredients. In Helmer's office, books and a chest. In the chest: **one of [[vlakith|Vlakith]]'s three dragon eggs**, and 1,800 gp. Sufos's share: 600 gp.

### The map opens

We mapped the corridors out from the warehouse. Four exits:

1. Back up to **CCC** itself.
2. A **stone by the oasis**.
3. **[[eshians-garden|Eshian's Garden]]** — Eshian's estate in the [[new-heights|New Heights]] district.
4. A **smith** at the **[[house-of-copper|House of Copper]]**.

The Eshian exit is suggestive. It does not, on its own, prove that the smart, manipulable wizard is a cultist. It could mean the cult has been using his estate without his knowledge. It could also mean what it looks like. We do not know yet, and we are recording it as ambiguous: the warehouse is rented either by Dolan Ward or by Eshian. Which one — unknown.

### Edegard. The smith. Shereen.

Deeper in the tunnels we found a man chained and tortured to within an inch of his life. He gave his name as **[[edegard|Edegard]]**. He was, he said, the man [[shereen|Shereen]] had been searching for. We sent for her at once.

We came up out of the tunnels at the smith's place at the [[house-of-copper|House of Copper]]. The smith was a cultist. We dealt with him.

Then [[shereen|Shereen]] came. We met her in the street and she took us with her to her temple — the **[[house-of-bahamut|House of Bahamut]]** — past the seven statues of the gods, down the stairs, into a private room.

### The truth, told to a dragon

This is where the world widened.

Tiamat thrives on chaos. The Dragon Cult uses **war** as a deliberate distraction — a cover of noise behind which Tiamat's resurrection moves. The Kayrun/Thalia war is not just Drow politics. It is *part of the resurrection's cover*.

The cult is led by **five Disciples**. Helmer, the local cell leader, was *under* one of them.

- **#1: [[gregory-white|Gregory White]]** — the senior Crimson Assembly mage in [[emond|Emond]]. He is *a white dragon*. Tiamat's white head, sitting at the head of her cult.
- **#2: [[dolan-ward|Grim / Dolan Ward]]** — Crimson Assembly member, in Emberstone for what must now look like a coronation of his life's work.
- **#3:** Unknown.
- **#4:** Unnamed. Centuries old. Of the old school. Cultivates the undead. [[helmer|Helmer]] was angling for this seat. Dolan dislikes the necromancer's ways, but Dolan is already #2 — he is not competing.
- **#5:** Unknown.

[[vlakith|Vlakith]] made a pact with [[tiamat|Tiamat]] long ago, when Tiamat still walked the material plane. Tiamat foresaw that she would be sealed in a prison. The pact gave the Githyanki access to red dragons — that alliance has shaped the planes ever since. **[[helmer|Helmer]] used this pact to obtain the three dragon eggs from Vlakith.**

The eggs are meant to hatch and grow into a dragon army. The army's purpose: overpower the Ashari to take **the Fire Orb and the Air Orb** — the last two. The cult **already has the Earth and the Water Orbs**. (This resolves the Water Orb status from Chapter 1 — it was lost.)

Of the three eggs: **we have one**. **[[dolan-ward|Dolan]] has one**. **[[gregory-white|Gregory White]] has one**.

But growing a dragon takes time the cult does not have. So Dolan is building a **machine** that ages dragons rapidly. We have seen this machine before — a prototype in the northern mountain we explored, and another in the prequel game when we played different characters infiltrating a dragon castle. The cult has been working on this for a very long time. The missing piece was always *time*. And now Dolan has found a way to obtain it.

The Drow have a magic technology that manipulates time. They use it on spider eggs to make them grow rapidly. Dolan has been hunting for this technology for years. He has finally established direct contact with the Drow who possess it. The Drow/Gruun deal that the city believes is a war alliance is, in fact, **the delivery channel for that technology**. With the Drow time-tech feeding the Dolan machine, eggs that would take years to mature become dragons in **days**, and some of those dragons will be large enough to lay new eggs. Exponential.

Dolan plans to use the resulting dragon force to take **the Rath area** within weeks — and within Rath sits **Vulca**, the city of the Fire Ashari. The same city my wedding to [[edna|Edna]] was meant to be in. This is the immediate target.

[[gruun|Gruun]] himself is **not part of the cult**. He is a Mind Flayer collaborator (already known) but not a Disciple. His personal cut of the Drow deal: **one of the dragons**. He wants a dragon of his own. That is why gold alone will not turn him.

### Murdoch's slip

In the telling of all this — and there was a great deal of telling — Murdoch let slip that Zeffel wants Gruun dead. To Shereen. We violated Zeffel's hard rule within hours of agreeing to it.

Shereen is allied. Her discretion may keep this contained. But the secret is one mouth wider than it was at breakfast, and that is on us.

### The plan

We weighed our options with Shereen for some time:

- ✗ Steal the Drow time-tech device — we don't even know where it is.
- ✗ Knock on Gruun's door — gives the Seekers and the Iron Ring a target.
- ✗ Take Helmer or Shereen north to face Dolan directly — wrong target, wrong time.
- ✓ **Shereen flies us, in dragon form, to the entrance of Gruun's office, and we enter the meeting and stop the deal directly.**

That is the plan.

### The doorway

We left the House of Bahamut. We began walking out of the temple grounds.

We saw the silhouette of [[dolan-ward|Dolan]] entering the temple with five other people behind him. He had the **scepter** in his hand. He was pointing it at [[shereen|Shereen]].

End of session.

## Discoveries

- **The five Disciples lead the Dragon Cult** — Gregory White (#1, white dragon, Emond), Dolan/Grim (#2, in Emberstone), an unnamed centuries-old necromancer (#4), and two unknowns. Almost certainly mapped to Tiamat's five chromatic heads.
- **The Crimson Assembly's leadership is the Dragon Cult's leadership** — Gregory White, Dolan's superior at the guild, is also Disciple #1. [[gordon-merek|Gordon Merek]] — our Crimson ally — has been investigating his own boss's lieutenant without knowing it.
- **The Vlakith–Tiamat pact** — Tiamat made it before her imprisonment, gave the Githyanki red dragons. Helmer used this pact to acquire the three eggs.
- **Two orbs already lost.** Earth (we knew) and Water (now confirmed). Fire and Air remain — and Fire is the next target.
- **The dragon-aging machine** — long-running cult project; the Drow time-magic is the missing catalyst. Without that delivery, Dolan's plan becomes a years-long problem. With it, days.
- **Gruun is not in the cult** — he's a beneficiary trying to buy a personal dragon. The deal is still the linchpin to stop, but for the world's sake, not because Gruun is one of *them*.
- **Aradin is dead** — confirmed by Azar. The CCC letter is from someone else who knows Ashari sign-language.
- **CCC = Carl's Caravan Company** — the cult's logistics front in Emberstone. Tunnels under the city connect it to Eshian's estate, the oasis, and the House of Copper smith.
- **The Wish reshaped Murdoch's history** — the Poetic Murderer is no longer real; alcoholism gone; some of his most haunting cases have simply ceased to exist.

## Closing reflection (out of character)

We could have reached out to the Fire Ashari for backup. We could have planned a coordinated assault with Zeffel. We could have brought Ryth into the underground rather than leaving him outside.

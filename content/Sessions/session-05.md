## Summary

I was a geologist before I was a soldier. I studied strata, fault lines, the places where pressure builds until something breaks. Three Waters was a fault line. We all felt it building from the moment we arrived. When it broke, it broke completely, and the aftershocks are still running through us.

The Drow came. The Dragon Cult came. We held the city.

We also killed [[leo|Leo]]'s brother, which is a sentence I need to sit with for longer than I have had time to sit with it.

Chapter 1 is over. I am writing this in an inn room in [[three-waters|Three Waters]] on the first quiet night in what feels like weeks, and I am not sure I know what chapter 2 will look like. But I know what it must contain: the Earth Orb. The other orbs. Tiamat, stopped.

The rest — all of it — is secondary to that.

## Key Events

The day before the invasion was a full day's preparation, and I threw myself into it completely. This is the part of war I understand and am good at: fortification. Structural assessment. Chokepoints, load-bearing walls, places where a small force can hold a larger one. [[three-waters|Three Waters]] is built around its water features, which gives it natural defensive advantages — defenders can force attackers to cross channels under fire — but its walls had not been designed with the kind of assault that was coming.

I spent the day with [[felix-thornewood|Felix Thornewood]]'s people, reinforcing gatehouse archways, widening murder holes, identifying where the weakest sections of wall could be braced with shaped stone from beneath. My hands ached at the end of it. Good ache. The ache of doing something that matters.

I also prayed. Longer than usual. I asked Moradin specifically for steadiness in the fighting that was coming — not victory, because I have learned that praying for victory is presumptuous; Moradin decides what victories cost and whether they are worth the price. I asked for steadiness. He gave me something harder: clarity.

The invasion began at dawn. First came the flying ship.

[[zuthan|Zuthan]]'s ship — the one we had watched being prepared from [[drageslottet|Drageslottet]]'s galleries and had chosen not to stop. I had not let myself think about whether that was the right call. I thought about it now.

The ship's commander stepped onto the deck as we closed with it. [[leo|Leo]] stopped moving. He had gone completely still, which is not a state I had ever seen him in before. I looked at the figure on the deck. I looked at [[leo|Leo]].

The commander was [[leos-brother|Leo's brother]].

I will not detail what followed. It is not mine to detail — it belongs to [[leo|Leo]] and to whatever grief he carries about the choices his family made or did not make. What I will write is that we fought, because there was no other outcome available to us. The ship had to be stopped. The army was two hours behind it.

He died in the fight. [[leo|Leo]] did not speak for a long time afterward.

There was no time for mourning. I hate that there was no time for mourning. Moradin's tradition holds that the dead deserve a full day of remembrance, the proper rites, the naming of their deeds — good and ill — before the stone closes over them. We gave [[leos-brother|Leo's brother]] nothing. We put him aside like a problem we would return to later and turned to face the army.

The battle was everything I had spent the day preparing for and more. Running engagements through the streets, defense of the gate, fighting on the water, fighting in the narrow lanes where a Dwarf with a shield and a fire-infused warhammer is at his most useful. [[moltres|Moltres]] earned its name that day. [[molton|Molton]] turned arrows and blades both. I blessed my Fire Ashari training — [[azar|Azar]] and [[moltar|Moltar]] had pushed me hard in those months, and I felt the results every time I planted my feet and held a position.

We held. The Drow army broke against [[three-waters|Three Waters]] like water against granite. Eventually they retreated.

I sat down on a stone step, alone, when it was over. My armor was dented. My hands were shaking again, but this time not from shame — from exhaustion, which is different. I looked at [[moltres|Moltres]] in my lap and thought about [[moltar|Moltar]] teaching me to shape the fire into it. I thought about Edna, who would hear about this eventually and worry. I thought about the Earth Orb, still missing, still waiting.

And I thought about [[leo|Leo]]'s face when he recognized his brother on that ship's deck.

## Discoveries

- [[three-waters|Three Waters]] can be defended, and was. The fortification approach worked.
- The Drow operation against [[three-waters|Three Waters]] was backed by the Dragon Cult — they were coordinating the ship as part of a combined assault. This means the Drow-cult alliance is formal, not opportunistic.
- The flying ship we chose not to sabotage in [[drageslottet|Drageslottet]] was the one that carried [[leos-brother|Leo's brother]]. I do not know what to do with this information morally. I have put it in a corner and will not take it out until I have more time.

## Open Threads

- The Earth Orb remains the primary mission. Orb location intelligence from the Divination is still valid but aging.
- [[leo|Leo]] lost his brother. There are no open threads about this in any tactical sense but it is the thing I will be watching: how does a man carry that, and does it change the man who carries it?
- What comes next? Chapter 1 ended with a victory, but the Dragon Cult still exists, still has one orb, still is organized and moving. The war is not won. The fault line has released pressure but it has not healed.
- I have been promised a posting with the Fire Ashari after this. I intend to honor that commitment. But the world has become considerably more complicated than a geological survey.

I will return to [[moltar|Moltar]]. I will tell him what I have found. I will continue the work.

Moradin, I am grateful for this day. Even the parts I cannot be grateful for. Even [[leos-brother|Leo's brother]] on that ship deck. Even all of it.

Let the stone remember it properly, since we did not have time to.

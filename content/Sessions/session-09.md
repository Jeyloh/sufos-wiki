## Summary

I am writing this in the [[underdark|Underdark]].

This is the first time in years I have been genuinely underground — not the comfortable deep of a mine with engineered passages, but real underdark: the unplanned geology of the world, passages formed by water and heat and time rather than by anything that intended to make them. The stone here is different from the [[emberstone|Emberstone]] sandstone above. Down here it is igneous — basalt predominantly, with intrusions of something darker that I would need a proper lamp to analyze. Old stone. The oldest stone I have been in since [[prides-call|Pride's Call]].

It feels, in a way that I am aware is irrational and do not apologize for, like coming home to a home that has been taken over by someone else in your absence. The stone is right. Everything else down here is wrong.

Moradin took my diamond tooth today. I will address that when I reach that part.

## Key Events

We took the wrong passage.

Someone had told us to go right from the [[embermines|Embermines]] entrance. We went center. This is the kind of decision that, when you are writing about it afterward from a position of having survived it, you recognize was a mistake. At the time it seemed reasonable, or at least nobody stopped it from happening.

We went hundreds of meters deep. [[murdoch|Murdoch]] stopped moving partway down. Magical paralysis, or something functionally indistinguishable from it — he simply would not advance. [[leo|Leo]] carried him. This was, I will note, one of the more genuinely decent things I have seen [[leo|Leo]] do, and I acknowledge it.

In a massive cavern at the bottom of the central passage, we encountered three devils. I want to be precise about this: they were not dressed as devils or performing devilhood theatrically. They were devils, with the specific spiritual weight that a cleric of Moradin recognizes immediately and that feels like trying to stand on a sloped stone surface. Wrong angle. Wrong gravity.

The most prominent one had glass wings — literally glass, or something like it, translucent and structured. He spoke calmly. He said he was a servant of [[zariel|Zariel]], who rules [[avernus|Avernus]], the first layer of the Nine Hells. He addressed [[murdoch|Murdoch]] specifically.

He said there is a shadow demon inside [[murdoch|Murdoch]]. Its name is [[orgaz|Orgaz]].

He offered to remove [[orgaz|Orgaz]] without destroying [[murdoch|Murdoch]]'s magical abilities — which would be the natural cost of a demon extraction — in exchange for a devil pact. The pact's terms were not fully specified, but pacts with servants of [[zariel|Zariel]] generally involve obligations in [[avernus|Avernus]] after death.

[[murdoch|Murdoch]] denied that the demon existed.

The glass-winged devil did not argue. He simply lit the torches in the cavern — all of them, simultaneously, a controlled demonstration of power — and [[orgaz|Orgaz]] manifested. I have seen shadow magic before. I have never seen a demon tear out of a person like a splinter being drawn from infected flesh while the wound is still open. It was enormous — a mass of shadow behind [[murdoch|Murdoch]], with weight and presence and the specific wrongness that evil spirits have when they are fully visible.

Then the devil left. His offer stands open, apparently. He will wait.

What followed was chaos. [[murdoch|Murdoch]] attempted to Banish [[orgaz|Orgaz]] and instead Banished himself — a mistake under pressure that I understand but which had immediately bad consequences, because [[orgaz|Orgaz]] then charmed [[murdoch|Murdoch]] when he returned and retreated back inside him. We were back where we started, except now [[murdoch|Murdoch]] was charmed by his own passenger and there had been a great deal of arguing.

I said what I needed to say: [[murdoch|Murdoch]] is walking around with a demon inside him and the party has been looking away from this for longer than it should have. I said this plainly and was told, in various ways, to be patient or to mind my own business. A demon in a traveling companion is not a thing I can categorize as someone else's business. I am a cleric of Moradin. Evil spirits in people I travel with are definitionally my area.

Five guards from the prison caught up with us and attacked. We killed them. [[orgaz|Orgaz]] drained the soul of the youngest one as he died. I watched it happen. The boy could not have been more than twenty.

I went to pray alone, which was probably the only thing that kept me from saying something permanently damaging to party cohesion.

Moradin appeared in vision. He did not look angry. He looked disappointed, which is worse. He reached into my mouth and removed the diamond tooth he gave me years ago when I earned my place as an Earth Ashari field agent. The diamond tooth is a mark of divine favor — it burns with a subtle internal light that only clerics can see, a constant reminder of who I serve and what I am. The removal of it is not a punishment exactly. It is a revoking of trust.

Then he spoke. Two things.

First: "Do better." That was all. Two words, carrying the weight of everything he had just shown me that he was disappointed by.

Second, and I was not expecting this: the most important goal is stopping Tiamat. He said this clearly and with urgency I was not prepared for. He said I must never lose sight of it. He said the ends can, in this specific pursuit, justify the means — that he understands we are fighting a war against something that would break the world, and that some latitude is necessary. But he also said I must conduct myself lawfully and righteously if I am to carry his power. Not perfectly. Lawfully and righteously. There is a difference.

I came back to the party somewhat steadied. They noticed the tooth was gone. I did not explain fully. "Moradin gave me a task," I said. That is accurate.

We found ruins of an old Githyanki structure in the deep passages — old enough that the stone had grown around the metal framework the way stone does, slowly consuming. Three [[umberhulks|Umberhulks]] in the surrounding chambers were among the worst fights I have been in since [[dolan-ward|Dolan Ward]]. The Deck curse is real: I felt the reduced statistics in every swing, every attempt to hold ground, every healing word I pushed through. I am working harder for results I used to achieve easily. Moradin said to do better. I am trying.

[[aradin|Aradin]]'s cell was empty when we breached it. He was not there. The alarm went off. [[leo|Leo]] killed a guard, who had rung the bell first. I said, "Do you always have to shoot first?" [[leo|Leo]] did not answer this question. We dove into the [[underdark|Underdark]] below to hide.

In the deep, we encountered [[duergar|Duergar]] patrols. I paid 600 gold for safe passage — a lot of money, but cheaper than fighting Duergar in their own territory while reduced by the Deck and exhausted from [[umberhulks|Umberhulks]]. Their leader [[grath|Grath]] gave me a medallion as proof of passage agreement. I respect the [[duergar|Duergar]] approach to negotiation: they want coin, you give them coin, everyone moves on. No theology required.

We are taking a long rest here, in a hollow in the basalt that I expanded slightly with stoneshaping. It is the most comfortable I have been in days.

## Discoveries

- [[orgaz|Orgaz]] is real. The demon is real. We saw it. [[murdoch|Murdoch]] is charmed by it and will not hear reason on this subject.
- [[zariel|Zariel]]'s devil (glass-winged) has left an open offer: remove [[orgaz|Orgaz]] cleanly in exchange for a devil pact. The offer remains standing.
- Moradin has revoked my diamond tooth. I must earn it back by doing better. He has also clarified that stopping Tiamat is the priority above all personal righteousness — but that I cannot abandon righteousness entirely or I lose the power to do the mission.
- [[aradin|Aradin]] was not in the cell. He escaped via the cave-in or was moved. He is still somewhere.
- The [[duergar|Duergar]] passage medallion from [[grath|Grath]] gives us some safety in this section of the [[underdark|Underdark]].

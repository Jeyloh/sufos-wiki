## Summary

Lolth spoke to me.

Not a vision. Not a divination. Directly, into my mind, with the specific intention of breaking something in me. She is very good at this — breaking things. Webs are built for catching and holding, not for crushing, and the Spider Queen has had ten thousand years to learn exactly which threads to pull.

I am writing this in the chamber where we destroyed the Aspect of [[lady-eovah|Lady Eovah]]. The party is unconscious or near it. We are bleeding from our ears and eyes, though this has stopped. [[ryth|Ryth]] has made a threat. [[elris|Elris]] is free. We won.

I do not feel like we won. I feel like a cliff face after an earthquake: the structure is intact but the geology has shifted, and it will take time to know what has actually changed and what has just been shaken.

I need to write while it is still in me, before the shaking settles into something I can pretend was less than it was.

## Key Events

Lolth's temple is the most wrong place I have been in, architecturally. Not wrong like a poorly constructed building — wrong like a place that was built for something it was never meant to be. The bones of [[crech-mediin|Crech Mediin]] are ancient Drow stone, competent and austere, the kind of stonework that respects its own load-bearing requirements. The temple has covered that stone with hundreds of layers of web — literal silk, so thick in places it blocked the passage light entirely, with the structural density of geological strata. Hundreds of giant spiders moved through it, many of them large enough that I could hear their joints working. And beneath the webs, the chasm.

The fight began over that chasm.

I want to be accurate about what I understood going in: Moradin had told me that adamantine and holy power would destroy the enemy's stones. We had Silence, which I cast immediately — if I could take the priest's spellcasting and the Drow's ability to coordinate vocally, I gave us more time before they overwhelmed us by numbers. We had [[moltres|Moltres]], consecrated and ready. And we were going to need to find and destroy three crystals — [[lady-eovah|Lady Eovah]]'s resurrection anchors — before she fully manifested.

The priest and his Drow were sending sacrifices into the chasm as offerings while we fought. I tried not to look directly at what was being sacrificed. I focused on the crystals.

The first portal — the first crystal, glowing with the particular cold purple that Lolth's divine power generates — I destroyed with [[moltres|Moltres]] and a sacred flame channel that I pushed harder than I have pushed anything since the Deck reduced me. Moradin's power through a compromised conduit is still Moradin's power. I felt it move through the curse like water through fractured stone: finding every channel, filling every crack. The crystal shattered. Good.

[[murdoch|Murdoch]] destroyed the second while I was still recovering from the first.

[[leo|Leo]] rushed the third. He went down before he reached it. He went down hard — the Aspect of [[lady-eovah|Lady Eovah]] was waking now, pulling herself through the final portal the way something pulls itself up from depth: slowly, fully, wrong. I healed [[leo|Leo]]. Used more than I had left to give. We both went down.

[[murdoch|Murdoch]] was keeping her attention. [[murdoch|Murdoch]], with [[orgaz|Orgaz]] inside him and the weight of everything the last several months have cost him, stood in front of a manifesting Aspect of Lolth and held her focus. I will never say this to him directly because it would be insufferable for both of us. But I am writing it here: that was brave.

Then [[elris|Elris]] came through the door.

He had gotten free — how, I do not yet know. He had healing potions. He used them on [[leo|Leo]] and me, both. I stood up on the second try.

[[ryth|Ryth]] fought beside us throughout the battle, his Githyanki steel finding weak points in the Aspect's form. His blows were decisive when she was weakened. The Aspect of [[lady-eovah|Lady Eovah]] fell. The portal collapsed. The webs in the ceiling began to fall.

Barely standing. Everyone with less than they started with. The crystals gone, the Aspect gone, [[crech-mediin|Crech Mediin]] technically retaken for the [[githyanki|Githyanki]]. Mission accomplished, in the specific technical sense that the objective has been achieved and everyone is alive.

Then [[ryth|Ryth]] turned on us.

He was not grateful. He was controlled, which is a different thing from hostile, but only slightly. He said: tell me what happened to Garax. He said it with the particular flatness of someone who has already decided what they will do if they do not like the answer.

[[garax|Garax]]. A red dragon. I went through everything I know about the dragons we have encountered: [[zephyrion|Zephyrion]], who we freed in [[emberstone|Emberstone]] in exchange for a future favor. Is [[garax|Garax]] [[zephyrion|Zephyrion]]? A different dragon? Something that happened to [[thraxus|Thraxus]] that I missed? The party told [[ryth|Ryth]] what we knew.

Then the darkness.

It was not natural darkness. Natural darkness is the absence of light. This was the presence of something — a weight in the air, a pressure in the stone beneath my feet, a wrongness that every geological instinct I have says should not be there. It flooded the chamber the way groundwater floods a mine shaft: everywhere at once, through every crack, no mercy.

It entered our minds.

Lolth.

I tried Divine Intervention. I called for Moradin with everything I had, full channel, the loudest I have ever been in prayer. Nothing came. Or nothing I could hear over the weight of her in my head.

She spoke to each of us. I can only speak to what she said to me.

She named my party. Murderers. She is not wrong that people die around us; she is wrong that this makes us murderers. Losers. She named the missions we have failed, the objectives unachieved, the people we did not save in time. Traitors. She named times the party's interests conflicted and someone chose wrongly; she named my own patience with [[leo|Leo]]'s violence as complicity, and she is not entirely wrong about that either. Evil. She named the devil freed from [[drageslottet|Drageslottet]]. She named [[orgaz|Orgaz]] in [[murdoch|Murdoch]] and the party's failure to address it. She named the guard who died at the mine bell.

*"Shall you be holy?!"*

She said it with contempt. As if the question is absurd. As if the distance between what I am and what I have sworn to be is so vast that the oath itself is the joke.

The words cut deep — I wrote that and then sat with it for a minute, because "cut deep" is an easy phrase and what I actually mean is: they found the crack. The crack that has been there since the Deck took my stats and Moradin took my tooth. The crack that opened when I walked into [[dolan-ward|Dolan Ward]]'s hall upright and overconfident and went down. The crack that formed the first time [[leo|Leo]] killed someone unnecessarily and I said my piece and was ignored and chose to continue anyway.

Lolth found the crack because she is very good at this and because the crack is real.

The darkness released. We were back in the chamber. Bleeding from eyes and ears, all of us except [[ryth|Ryth]], who had something blocking her access. The webs were settling. The spiders had scattered. The crystals were ash.

I am sitting on old Drow stone that has been underground for longer than any surface civilization has existed, and I am writing in a journal by firelight, and I am thinking about the crack.

Here is what I know about cracks in stone: they are not failure. A crack in stone is a record — it tells you the direction and intensity of a stress, it tells you what the stone survived, it tells you where the material was tested. A crack that is identified, understood, and properly addressed becomes part of the stone's history without ending its usefulness. A crack that is ignored spreads.

Lolth found my crack and tried to drive a wedge into it. She is the Spider Queen; she works in threads and leverage, pulling until things give way.

Stone does not give way to threads. Not good stone. Not Dwarven stone. Not Moradin's stone.

I am the crack and I am also the stone around it. She does not get to decide which one I am.

*"Shall you be holy?!"*

Yes.

Not because I am perfect. Not because the party is righteous or the methods are clean or the casualties have all been justified. But because Moradin did not ask me to be holy among people who made it easy. He asked me to do this work, in this place, with these people, and remain Moradin's cleric throughout. The crack is not a contradiction of the oath. It is evidence that the oath is being tested.

Tested stone is stronger stone.

I am going to get off this floor, check on my party, have a serious conversation with [[ryth|Ryth]] about [[garax|Garax]], and then find a way out of [[crech-mediin|Crech Mediin]] that does not involve the [[black-lake|Black Lake]] tentacles.

## Summary

We escaped [[drageslottet|Drageslottet]]. We reached the surface. We made contact with an agent of the [[crimson-assembly|Crimson Assembly]] who turned out to be, against all probability, helpful. We were teleported to a palace. I found a library.

It has been a day of extraordinary information, and I find myself sitting in a bathhouse in [[emond|Emond]] — the queen's city, they call it, the Marble Halls — writing by candlelight with wet hair and a stone sample from the palace courtyard in my breast pocket, trying to organize everything I have learned.

There is a great deal to organize.

## Key Events

Sneaking out of the castle was less dignified than I would prefer to record. Two guards died. I do not apologize for that — they were Dragon Cult, and they would have returned the favor without hesitation — but I mention it because I made myself look at each of them as we stepped over them. I do not look away from the consequences of combat. Moradin expects me to know the weight of what I do.

We then encountered [[gordon-merek|Gordon Merek]].

He was simply there, in a corridor that should have been empty, visible only because he chose to become visible. He had been watching us, he said. He had been curious. He was, he explained, a member of the [[crimson-assembly|Crimson Assembly]] — the same organization as [[dolan-ward|Dolan Ward]], which made every hand in the party go to a weapon simultaneously. But he was not Dolan Ward's ally. He was suspicious of him. He was investigating him.

There is a proverb in Dwarven stonework: when you core-sample a rock face, sometimes you find a seam of ore you did not expect, running in a completely different direction from the surrounding geology. [[gordon-merek|Gordon Merek]] is that seam. He should not be trustworthy. The evidence suggests he is.

We shared everything with him. Perhaps that was reckless. But he already knew enough about us to have reported us if that was his intention. We told him about the orbs, about the dream, about the cult's plan to converge at [[blade-garden|Blade Garden]]. He listened without interrupting, which is a quality I appreciate in a person.

He told us one thing that chilled me: a dragon has been hunting the prisoners we freed from the villa. Those people — the commoners, Lord Therris's daughter — were being tracked. I do not know if they made it to the Saints' tavern before the dragon found them. I have been trying not to think about it. I am failing at that.

Then [[dolan-ward|Dolan Ward]]'s spider-machine — some kind of mechanical detection device, I could feel the arcane vibration of it in the stone itself — found us. We had seconds.

[[gordon-merek|Gordon Merek]] cast a teleportation and we arrived in the courtyard of the queen's palace in [[emond|Emond]].

The palace stonework is extraordinary. White marble primarily, with gold inlay at the load-bearing points — which is architecturally useless but visually compelling. The craftspeople here understand proportion. They do not always understand structural necessity, but they understand beauty, and in a surface city that counts for something.

I separated from the group immediately. I had a message to send.

I found a mage in the palace district willing to perform a long-distance Sending for coin — [[aghmar|Aghmar]], older, wore too many rings, but competent. I composed the message carefully: cryptic enough that if it was intercepted it would reveal nothing useful, specific enough that [[moltar|Moltar]] would understand. My Earth Ashari leader. My mentor. The man who helped me make [[moltres|Moltres]] and [[molton|Molton]] in the forge below Fire Ashari's station.

His reply came within the hour: he would contact "our brothers at the water." The Water Ashari. And I was to continue hunting the Earth Orb. That is clarity. That is mission. I held onto it.

Then I went to the library.

The palace library in [[emond|Emond]] is substantial for a surface institution. Not Dwarven — nothing in a surface library is ever quite what a Dwarven archive is — but well-organized and deep in places. I found two books that matter enormously.

The first: *Dragons and Disciples*, which discusses the [[knights-of-the-eclipse|Knights of the Eclipse]] and a black dragon called [[crulecs|Crulecs]]. I took notes but the full significance is not yet clear to me.

The second: *Gods of War and Peace* by [[ulrich-eben|Ulrich Eben]], who was once Archmage of the [[crimson-assembly|Crimson Assembly]] before he apparently wrote this book and disappeared or was removed from his position. The book is a hidden-room find — a concealed shelf behind a standard geography section, which told me someone did not want this information generally available.

The book explains everything.

Four Elemental Orbs. Moradin is listed among the primordial gods who created or empowered them — this I had suspected but never seen confirmed in a primary source. The Ashari are the guardians, which I knew. Tiamat's plan to use her dragons to seize all four orbs and use them for... something. The book is careful not to say exactly what. I believe the book does not know exactly what, which is a more frightening kind of careful.

I performed a [[divination|Divination]] in the corner of the library, on the cold stone floor, [[moltres|Moltres]] laid across my knees. Moradin answered. He gave me the locations of all four orbs. I have written them down in the cipher that [[moltar|Moltar]] taught me when I was still an apprentice, on the inside cover of the geography book I used as cover.

This is the most important intelligence I have gathered since arriving on the surface.

I rejoined the party at the bathhouse they had found, which turned out to be the finest establishment in [[emond|Emond]]. I did not object to this. The mineral content of the water is excellent, actually — high calcium and magnesium, volcanic origin. I soaked for an hour and tried to let my shoulders unknot.

We met [[percy-andare|Percy Andare]] here and someone named [[elris|Elris]]. The circle grows. We stayed the night at an inn called The Queen's Nod.

[[gordon-merek|Gordon Merek]] found us the next morning with information about a family called the Thornewood and a crisis we were apparently now involved in.

## Discoveries

- *Gods of War and Peace* by [[ulrich-eben|Ulrich Eben]] confirms the theological and cosmological nature of the four Elemental Orbs. Moradin is connected to their creation. The Ashari are their intended guardians.
- Tiamat's plan involves using dragons to seize the orbs. What she does with them when she has all four is not clearly stated but is apparently catastrophic.
- **Moradin confirmed all four orb locations via Divination.** This information is time-sensitive and encoded.
- When the Dragon Cult acquires a second orb, convergence happens at [[blade-garden|Blade Garden]]. This remains the critical threshold.
- [[gordon-merek|Gordon Merek]] has given us a communication device. We can reach him.
- The Water Ashari have been contacted via [[moltar|Moltar]]. Something may be coordinating on a larger scale.
